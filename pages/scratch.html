Fly Fishing: For Starters

What is it?

Fly fishing is a method of fishing that uses a lightweight artificial fly to imitate insects, baitfish, or other natural food that fish eat. Instead of relying on the weight of the lure, anglers use the momentum of a specially weighted line to cast the fly across the water. It’s a skillful and peaceful style of fishing that blends technique, observation, and timing to connect with fish in rivers, lakes, and streams. Here's why I love it:

  • I love the challenge of reading water, matching hatches, and learning the habits of fish.
  • Every trip feels like an adventure and a chance to explore new rivers and beautiful places.
  • It helps me slow down, stay present, and appreciate nature.
